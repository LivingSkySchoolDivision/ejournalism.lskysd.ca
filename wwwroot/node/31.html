Jamie McKenzie Keynote Address

Jamie McKenzie

 

 

This morning,
Cut Knife High School students Mena, Shay and Taylor took in Dr. Jamie McKenzie’s keynote address at the E-merging Learning Summit. Dr. McKenzie was introduced by Carleen Walker from Prairie Spirit School Division, and he delivered a great presentation about multiple literacies. The following are
Taylor’s notes and impressions from the presentation.

 

  • Our presentation started with an address from the ministry of education. The ministry is planning to invest more than 4 million to advance infrastructure
  • This funding will provide access and learning opportunities to students as well as teachers
  • In her introduction to Dr.McKenzie, Carleen Walker told the conference attendees that this speaker focuses on question formation and improving student thinking skills.
  • Dr. McKenzie is a graduate of Yale, and has spoken extensively around the world, from Singapore, to
    Sweden    .
  • He is the editor of From Now On, an educational technology website.
  • Dr. McKenzie says, “It only makes sense to have rich info in class room if you are going to let them ask questions”
  • Dr. McKenzie’s website includes information on his keynote from today—Reading and Comprehending Across a Dozen Information Literacies. The link to From Now On is: http://fno.org?mar09/dozen.html
  • He spoke about how literacy is more than reading the words on a page—it is about the ability to comprehend a painting, photograph, video, someone’s face.
  • Dr. McKenzie spoke a little about his life in his presentation; he’s 63, is friends with Bill Gates and is a marathon runner. One of the photos he included in his keynote today was from
    Paris    , where he ran the marathon last year. Dr. McKenzie is friends with Bill Gates, and has many ideas about politics and education; he mentioned that he is disappointed with previous political parties in the United States who have introduced the No Child Left Behind legislation. Dr. McKenzie has his own website addressing the legislation; it is called No Child Left, and the link is:     http://nochildleft.com/2006/dec06petition.html.
  • Dr. McKenzie spoke about twelve different literacies that all teachers should encourage their students to develop:

1.      natural

2.      organizational

3.      media

4.      emotional

5.      text

6.      social/cultural

7.      wthical

8.      numerical

9.      artistic

10.  visual

11.  scientific

12.  environmental

 

  • He showed the audience the Dove commercial (called Dove Evolution)- this showed a video about a young lady getting a makeover and they stretched her neck and made it very different from how they looked when she started- not her natural beauty and really has nothing to do with Dove!
  • Next, he showed a parody of a Dove commercial (called Slob Evolution)- it took a good looking man and turned him to unattractive- he had lots of beer, cigarettes, food, and got really fat- they reversed the way commercials are usually made- funny!
  • Dr. McKenzie gave the audience an opportunity to discuss what they had seen, and to develop questions about the material; some questions included:
    1. Who decides what’s beautiful?
    2. Is it really beautiful if you have to photoshop it?
    3. What is real?
    4. How would you compare naturally-occurring beauty with person-created beauty?
  • People use photoshop to make people look better
  • Dr. McKenzie spent some time in his presentation discussing politics and education; he said that most governments think that if they just test all the time, it will fix schools and improve learning.
  • He says that we live in a society where there is no time to “chew” on anything, but we need to learn to take our time with knowledge. Not all learning happens smoothly; he says that, “sometimes we stumble to understanding”.
  • Dr. McKenzie also discussed his family; his wife teaches Grade Five, and she says that the kids trained her to be a good teacher. She learned from her mistakes.
  • He says that we must learn not to “scoop and smoosh” our information, but should learn to distill comprehending- considering-wandering- wondering- pondering and they all point to each to each other.
  • Lateral thinking- www.visualthesaurus.com- shows many different words and you can click on the other words it goes to words for that word; he says that “we want our kids to learn how to wander”
  • Showed ten pictures and the audience had to decide which picture has the most beauty- pictures came from a folk festival in
    Seattle
  • Different result for an adult audience rather than a youth audience. Girls like the pictures of the young boys, whereas the choices of an older audience were very different!
  • In the 60’s there were no pictures on the computer- they had to go get them developed, and the availability of information was much more limited.
  • Dr. McKenzie also referenced his petition on No Child Left; Texas was doing testing, and they showed that they tried to make people smarter than they really were and they got caught- they gave kids copies of the test so they knew what was on the test and they did well on that but not other tests.
  • Kids need to know how to read documents right and not just sign things not knowing  what the conditions are applied; he used mortgage documents as an example of how people often read information and don’t really understand what they are taking in. No matter what school you attended, you need to know how to interpret information; even the best-educated people in the world can end up knowing nothing!
  • Jamie involved the audience a tremendous amount during his presentation, and was very enjoyable; watch for a video synopsis of this presentation on the website.
